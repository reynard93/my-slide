# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Internal team: engineers, technical leads, and collaborators who author and view decks in focused development environments. Audience arrives with context — these aren't cold pitches, they're communication tools between people who share a codebase or a problem space.

## Product Purpose

A slide authoring workspace where each deck is a crafted artifact. The goal is not to produce slides fast — it's to produce slides that feel authored: precise, readable, technically credible, and visually quiet enough that the content is always the subject.

Success looks like: an internal audience that trusts what they're looking at before the first word is spoken. Design earns attention rather than asking for it.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Minimal. Authored.

Voice is direct and confident without being loud. The workspace itself is invisible — it disappears and what remains is the deck.

## References

Vercel's dark-native aesthetic: clean dark surfaces, tight typographic hierarchy, monospace as a material (not decoration), product-grade polish in every component. Specific things to carry forward: how Vercel uses near-black backgrounds with high-chroma accent sparingly; how monospace anchors technical content without over-coding the whole page; how spacing creates hierarchy without ornament.

## Anti-references

- **Colorful SaaS slides**: gradient splashes, hero-metric panels, rainbow accent ramps. Generic conference-booth feel regardless of content quality.
- **Corporate PowerPoint**: blue + white, bullet lists, clip-art-era defaults. Safe but forgettable.
- **Startup pitch clichés**: 01/02/03 eyebrow section labels, identical card grids, numbered scaffolding used reflexively rather than because sequence carries information.
- **Over-animated**: entrance animations on everything, slide transitions that compete with the content for attention. Motion has to earn its place.

## Design Principles

1. **Typography carries the weight.** Scale, weight contrast, and letter-spacing do all the structural work. Decoration is the exception, not the scaffolding.
2. **Dark by conviction.** Not dark "because technical things look dark." Dark because these decks are authored and viewed in focused, low-ambient-light environments by people who spend most of their day in editors and terminals. The environment earns the choice.
3. **Content density, not content inflation.** Internal audiences have context — don't pad. Let high-value information breathe; let low-value information get cut.
4. **Authored, not templated.** Each deck has a design identity appropriate to its content. Consistent craft; not cloned structure across every slide.
5. **Motion earns its place.** Animation serves comprehension and pacing, never spectacle. When in doubt, cut it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Dark-first means contrast needs active care: body text ≥4.5:1 against dark backgrounds, accent colors checked before use as text. Reduced-motion support on every animation (crossfade or instant fallback). No content conveyed by color alone.
